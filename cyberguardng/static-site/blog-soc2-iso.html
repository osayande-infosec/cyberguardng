SOC 2 vs ISO 27001: Which Should You Choose?

Both SOC 2 and ISO 27001 are leading security frameworks. Choosing the right one depends on your customers, industry, and growth plans.

What is SOC 2?

SOC 2 is an attestation report focused on demonstrating that your controls meet criteria for security, availability, confidentiality, processing integrity, and privacy. It is widely adopted in North America, particularly in the SaaS and technology ecosystem.

Best for

  • SaaS and cloud service providers.
  • Technology companies selling to US-based customers.
  • Organizations needing a flexible, trust-focused report.

What is ISO 27001?

ISO 27001 is a global standard for building and maintaining an information security management system (ISMS). It focuses on governance, risk management, and continuous improvement.

Best for

  • Organizations with global customers or operations.
  • Businesses that value structured governance and formal certification.
  • Enterprises and regulated industries.

Key Differences

  • Type: SOC 2 is an audit report; ISO 27001 is a certification.
  • Scope: SOC 2 focuses on defined trust service criteria; ISO 27001 covers an ISMS across the organization.
  • Geography: SOC 2 is strongest in North America; ISO 27001 has broad international recognition.
  • Flexibility: SOC 2 can be tailored to your environment; ISO 27001 follows a structured control set.

Which Should You Choose?

If most of your customers are in North America and ask for a SOC 2 report, start there. If you operate globally or sell into regions where ISO standards are widely expected, ISO 27001 may be the better anchor. In some cases, mature organizations pursue both.
