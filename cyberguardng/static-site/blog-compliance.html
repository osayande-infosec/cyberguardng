Compliance as a Service Explained

Compliance-as-a-Service (CaaS) gives businesses a way to stay continuously audit-ready without building a full internal compliance function.

Why Compliance is Changing

Traditional compliance programs rely on point-in-time audits and static documentation. Modern cloud environments and customer expectations move too quickly for that model. Stakeholders want continuous proof that controls are working as intended.

What CaaS Delivers

  • Automated monitoring of key controls.
  • Evidence collection throughout the year instead of a last-minute scramble.
  • Policy and procedure guidance aligned to frameworks like SOC 2 and ISO 27001.
  • Support for risk assessments and remediation tracking.

Who Benefits Most

SaaS companies, healthcare providers, fintechs, professional services, and any business that stores or processes customer data in the cloud benefit from a more modern approach to compliance.
